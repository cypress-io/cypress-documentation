# Enterprise Reporting

**Enterprise Reporting is a Cypress Cloud feature that aggregates test data from every project in your
organization into a single, cross-project view.** It lets you track usage, spot trends and anomalies,
monitor flaky tests, and govern your entire testing program from one place—without manually stitching
together data from dozens of individual project dashboards.

As your organization grows, so does the challenge of understanding the health of your test suites.
Individual project dashboards tell you how _one_ project is doing, but they can't answer the questions
that engineering leaders and platform teams actually care about: _Is testing improving or degrading
across the company? Which teams are adopting Cypress effectively? Where is flake quietly eroding
developer trust and burning CI minutes? Are we getting a return on our testing investment?_ Enterprise
Reporting answers these questions.

With Enterprise Reporting you can:

- **See the whole picture.** Aggregate test results, usage, flake, and configuration across all
  projects, then drill down to a specific team, project, branch, or time period when something needs
  attention.
- **Catch problems before they spread.** Surface rising flake rates, failing builds, and inconsistent
  setups early—while they're still cheap to fix.
- **Govern with confidence.** Verify that teams are on supported Cypress versions, using smart
  orchestration, and following consistent practices across the organization.
- **Demonstrate impact.** Quantify how testing usage and quality are trending over time to justify
  investment and show progress to stakeholders.
- **Own your data.** Download the raw data behind any chart, or pull it programmatically into your own
  analytics platform with the [data extract API](/cloud/integrations/data-extract-api).

## How to access and use Enterprise Reporting

If available in your Cypress plan, Enterprise Reporting is accessible by clicking on the **Enterprise
Reporting** link in the left menu of Cypress Cloud.

Insights are organized into tabs—**Overall Usage**, **Test Results**, **Test Setup**, **Test
Execution**, **Flaky Tests**, and **Raw Data**—each answering a different category of question about
your testing program. Every tab presents interactive visual cards, and every card can be filtered by
date range, project, team, and branch, so you can move fluidly from an organization-wide summary down
to a single team or project. When the page first loads, data is displayed for all projects in your
organization for the current month and the preceding two months.

**A couple of things to note:**

- Data shown in Enterprise Reporting is not real-time. It is available as of midnight UTC of the current day.
  I.e. - data will be populated through "end of day yesterday".
- Historical data is available as specified by your data retention limit in Cypress Cloud.

The type of information and insights available on the various tabs are described in the sections that follow.

### Overall Usage

Understand how Cypress is being adopted across your organization and whether that adoption is growing,
plateauing, or concentrated in a handful of teams. This is the tab to reach for when you need to track
consumption against your plan, plan capacity, or report on adoption to stakeholders. It answers
questions like:

- How many Cypress tests are you utilizing across all projects in your organization?
- Where are those tests concentrated?
- How has test utilization changed over time?

Utilize the `Start Date` and `End Date` filters if you need to drill into a specific time period. The `Projects`
filter can be used to filter the data by one or multiple projects. The `Branch` filter can be used to select one or more
branches that exist in your projects. The data on this tab correlates to what Cypress Cloud
[counts as a test result](/cloud/faq#What-counts-as-a-test-result).

### Test Results

Get a clear, organization-wide read on test health and pinpoint where failures are concentrated. The
**Test Results** tab shows you how many of your runs, specs, or individual tests are passing or
failing—at all three levels of granularity—so you can compare projects, identify the teams that need
support, and confirm that quality is trending in the right direction.

### Test Setup

Enforce consistency and best practices across teams. The **Test Setup** tab shows how your runs are
configured and maintained, making it easy to spot projects that are out of date, missing performance
optimizations, or drifting from your organization's standards. It answers questions like:

- What is your proportion of end-to-end tests to [component tests](/app/component-testing/get-started)?
- How has the size of your test suites changed over time across your organization?
- What projects are configured and using Cypress smart orchestration capabilities?
- Which version of Cypress are you using for your runs?

Use these insights to drive upgrades, encourage adoption of smart orchestration to speed up CI, and
keep configuration consistent across the organization.

### Test Execution

Understand the environments your tests run in and keep CI fast and reliable. The **Test Execution**
tab surfaces the details around your Cypress runs so you can protect coverage, control execution time,
and benchmark individual projects against organization-wide norms. It answers questions like:

- Which browsers are being tested?
- Are the browsers used for testing consistent across your organization or changing over time within a project?
- Have there been changes to the test execution times of your Cypress runs?
- What is the average failure or error rate of tests across your organization and how do individual projects compare to those averages?

Use these insights to catch slowdowns before they frustrate developers and to ensure critical browsers
aren't being dropped from your coverage.

### Flaky Tests

Flake is one of the most expensive and trust-eroding problems in any test suite—it wastes CI minutes,
slows developers down, and trains teams to ignore failures. The **Flaky Tests** tab makes flake
visible and measurable across your entire organization so you can target it where it hurts most.

View flaky test occurrences and rates for the whole organization or specific projects, as a single
snapshot based on the start and end date filters or as a trend over time. Flake _rate_ is especially
useful because it shows what % of runs in a project are flaky, allowing you to compare projects fairly
regardless of how many runs each one has—so you can prioritize the projects where flake is doing the
most damage.

### Download data from Cloud

All of the charts shown in Enterprise Reporting allow you to download the raw data used to
render the chart visual. If you would like to manually download the raw data for further review, simply
hover over the chart, select `Download results` from the three ellipsis menu in the upper right corner of the
chart, and a menu will be displayed that allows you to download the data in the following formats:

- .csv
- .xlsx
- .json
- .png

Once you select the format you want to download, the file will immediately be downloaded by your browser.

### Retrieve your test data through API

The Cypress Cloud [data extract API](/cloud/integrations/data-extract-api) provides full access to
the test data shown within the Cloud Enterprise Reporting pages. Examples of the granular data are
provided on the **Raw Data** tab so that you can review the data before you decide to integrate with the
Cypress Cloud API. To download, simply hover over the chart, select `Download results`, and choose the
download format of your data. When you are ready to integrate with the API you can visit the
[data extract API](/cloud/integrations/data-extract-api) page for the list of endpoints, data
returned, and instructions for how to integrate.
